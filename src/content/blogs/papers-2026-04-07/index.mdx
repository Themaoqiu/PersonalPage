  summary={"论文指出当前视觉语言模型在需要细粒度视觉理解的任务中失效，原因是训练流程将视觉信息压缩至语言空间，导致模型只能处理可被映射到语义概念的实体。作者通过语义、形状和人脸的视觉对应任务验证：当相关实体可命名时模型表现显著优于不可命名的情况。进一步利用 logit lens 机制分析发现模型在可命名实体上会显式生成更唯一的对应 token，指示其依赖文本锚而非纯视觉表征。为了打破这一限制，研究者尝试为未知实体教授任意名称，性能提升；而任务特定微调则在不依赖语言先验的前提下进一步提高泛化能力。实验显示此类训练手段可以缓解 VLM 在视觉对应和新颖实体推理上的缺陷，从而揭示问题源自训练捷径而非基础架构。"}

## Thinking with Images

  arxivUrl={"https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02870"}
  hjfyUrl={"https://hjfy.top/arxiv/2604.02870"}
  summary={"论文考察了可视化大模型在相邻视角理解中的脆弱性，提出用 token 而不是像素进行视角扭曲。方法是构造目标视角上的密集网格，并对每个点执行后向 token 扭曲，以稳定地映射回源视角的 ViT token，从而保持语义一致性。实验在作者构建的 ViewBench 基准上展开，结果显示该 token 级扭曲在不同视角下都能可靠推理，优于像素扭曲、微调模型乃至生成扭曲方法。论文通过对比正向与后向扭曲，还进一步确认了后向方法在保持语义与结构完整性方面的优势。"}

<PaperCard
  title={"Xpertbench: Expert Level Tasks with Rubrics-Based Evaluation"}
  arxivUrl={"https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02368"}
  hjfyUrl={"https://hjfy.top/arxiv/2604.02368"}
  summary={"As Large Language Models (LLMs) exhibit plateauing performance on conventional benchmarks, a pivotal challenge persists: evaluating their proficiency in complex, open-ended tasks characterizing genuine expert-level cognition. Existing frameworks suffer from narrow domain coverage, reliance on generalist tasks, or self-evaluation biases. To bridge this gap, we present XpertBench, a high-fidelity benchmark engineered to assess LLMs across authentic professional domains. XpertBench consists of 1,346 meticulously curated tasks across 80 categories, spanning finance, healthcare, legal services, education, and dual-track research (STEM and Humanities). These tasks are derived from over 1,000 submissions by domain experts--including researchers from elite institutions and practitioners with extensive clinical or industrial experience--ensuring superior ecological validity. Each task uses detailed rubrics with mostly 15-40 weighted checkpoints to assess professional rigor. To facilitate scalable yet human-aligned assessment, we introduce ShotJudge, a novel evaluation paradigm that employs LLM judges calibrated with expert few-shot exemplars to mitigate self-rewarding biases. Our empirical evaluation of state-of-the-art LLMs reveals a pronounced performance ceiling: even leading models achieve a peak success rate of only ~66%, with a mean score around 55%. Models also exhibit domain-specific divergence, showing non-overlapping strengths in quantitative reasoning versus linguistic synthesis.. These findings underscore a significant \"expert-gap\" in current AI systems and establish XpertBench as a critical instrument for navigating the transition from general-purpose assistants to specialized professional collaborators."}
